    RhizomeChat

    Rhizome AI is a design research project and proof of concept designed to emerging paradigms for AI enabled user interfaces. The dominant paradigm for AI user interfaces is characterized by the pervasive chatbot model. The proliferation of these models has allowed for easy and rapid adoption of chatbots, but also present some significant limitations in how AI is used and interpreted. We propose a new exploratory paradigm for AI interfaces - Rhizomatic Interfaces - that move beyond linear text-based chatbot models. The “call and response” linear structure of most chatbots can make them hard to navigate, and difficult to recognize deeper patterns in the interrelationship between questions and ideas. The search function of Rhizome AI has two modes: Rhizome mode and Tree (arborescent) mode. A rhizomatic structure describes a non-hierarchical, interconnected structure that spreads in multiple directions like roots or networks, with no single central origin or authority or with any single point of entry or destination (i.e. in medias res). An arborescent or tree structure describes a hierarchical, tree-like structure that grows from a single trunk or central point into ordered, branching levels. This interface allows the user to toggle between these modes, and see the interrelationships between concepts in different ways. The current prototype uses Wikipedia content, as well as the hyperlinks between articles, to structure the user experience.
